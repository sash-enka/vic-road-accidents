Victorian Road Accidents in 2020

Lets explore 2020's Victorian road incident data


Victorian Road Accidents - Interactive Map

Interactive map above displays the location of ll Victorian road incidents in 2020. The size of the circle represents the number of people involved in the incident. The larger the circle, the more people were involved. The colour of the circle represents the type of incident that took place. Click on an accident type via the legend to further filter the visualisation. Additionally, you are able to use the Zoom toggle to get a clearer view of the accidents, as you Zoom into Melbourne city - the locality with the highest number of road accidents in 2020.


Number of Accidents by Accident Type

The colour densities in the above interactive map shows us that the most common types of accidents were collisions with otheer vehicles, and collisions with fixed objects. The following stacked area chart further elaborates on this by showing you the exact distribution of accident types, along with a timeframe, in months.


Number of Accidents by Region

This pie chart displays the distribution of Victorian road accidents on the separate regions. It is made clear with this visualisatio that the Metropolitan North West, and Metropolitan South East regions have a disproportionate amount of car accidents compared to the other regions. Reasons for this could be the fact that these areas are more populated and have well established transport infrastructure; putting more people on their roads and transport network.


LGAs with the Highest Number of Accidents

The following visualisation shows us the spread of accidents across the different LGAs. As you can see, the LGAs that have the highest nuber of road accidents are Melbourne, Casey, Geelong, Hume, Dandenong and Monash. Several factors can contribute to this outcome, including the population and area of the relevant LGA.
